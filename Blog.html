Week One

But correspondents say any decision on military support from Washington could hinge on political changes in Iraq. US Vice-President Joe Biden discussed possible "additional measures" that could help "roll back the terrorists' advances" with Iraqi Prime Minister Nouri Maliki on Wednesday, but also the need for national unity. The Obama administration has shown signs of frustration with Mr Maliki - a Shia Muslim who has long been accused of discriminating against the Sunni Arab minority community and monopolising power. Defence Secretary Chuck Hagel told a congressional hearing: "This current government in Iraq has never fulfilled the commitments it made to bring a unity government together with the Sunnis, the Kurds, and the Shia."
